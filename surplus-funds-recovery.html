Surplus Funds Recovery

Who This Service Is For.

Surplus funds recovery is available to a defined set of claimants. Below are the most common situations we assist with — each evaluated based on applicable records and the specific circumstances of your claim.

Former Property Owners

If your property was sold through a tax sale, foreclosure, or similar proceeding and the sale generated more than what was owed, you may be entitled to the remaining balance. We help former owners identify and pursue those unclaimed funds before they are permanently absorbed by the state.

Eligibility depends on applicable records and claim circumstances.

Heirs & Authorized Representatives

When a property owner has passed away, their heirs, estate executors, or legally authorized representatives may have a valid right to claim surplus funds on behalf of the estate. We work carefully with documentation requirements to support proper claim submission and verification.

Proper legal authorization and supporting documentation required.

Organizations with a Valid Claim Interest

Certain entities — including lienholders, creditors, or organizations with a documented legal interest in a property at the time of sale — may qualify to recover a portion of surplus proceeds. We assess your organization's standing and assist with preparing the necessary claim documentation.

Claim interest must be verifiable through applicable public records.

Not sure if you qualify? Every situation is unique. Reach out to our team for a straightforward assessment of your claim circumstances — no obligation, no jargon.

Documentation Checklist

What You Need to Provide.

Gathering the right information upfront helps us move your surplus funds claim forward efficiently and accurately. Below is a clear overview of what we'll need from you — nothing more, nothing less.

Claimant Identity

  • Government-issued photo ID Driver's license, state ID, or passport
  • Social Security Number (last 4 digits) For identity verification purposes only
  • Date of birth As it appears on your official identification
  • Proof of name changes (if applicable) Marriage certificate, court order, or legal decree

Relation to Property or Funds

  • Prior property address or parcel number The address associated with the foreclosure or sale
  • Proof of ownership or interest Deed, title, or mortgage statement showing your name
  • Heir or estate documentation (if applicable) Letters testamentary, probate records, or affidavit of heirship

Case & Reference Information

  • Court case number (if known) Found on foreclosure notices or court correspondence
  • Trustee sale or auction reference number Available on your Notice of Trustee's Sale
  • Any correspondence from the county or court Letters, notices, or statements referencing surplus funds

Mailing & Contact Information

  • Current mailing address Where you'd like all correspondence and documents sent
  • Primary phone number and email For status updates and follow-up questions
  • Preferred disbursement method Check by mail or direct deposit details (if available)

Don't worry if you don't have every document on hand — our team will guide you through each step and help identify what's available. We work with individuals, estates, and sole proprietors to gather what's needed efficiently and with care.
