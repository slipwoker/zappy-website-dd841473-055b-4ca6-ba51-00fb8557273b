Document Checklist

What the Client Needs to Provide.

To prepare your compliance documents accurately and on time, we ask that you gather the following information before your consultation. Having these materials ready allows us to work efficiently and ensures that every filing — whether for a sole proprietor, LLC, or established organization — reflects your situation precisely and meets Washington State requirements.

  • Existing Business or Organization Records

    Any current registration certificates, articles of incorporation, operating agreements, or prior state filings that document the legal history of your entity.

  • Prior Filing References & Confirmation Numbers

    Previous UBI numbers, Secretary of State filing IDs, or any agency confirmation codes tied to prior submissions. These help us verify your standing and avoid duplication.

  • Full Legal Business Name & Trade Names

    The exact registered legal name of your business, any DBA (doing business as) names currently in use, and your preferred name if you are looking to register a business name as a sole proprietor for the first time.

  • Business Type & Structure Details

    Whether your entity is a sole proprietorship, partnership, LLC, corporation, or nonprofit. Include state of formation and whether you operate in Washington or are registering as a foreign entity.

  • Names & Roles of Key Individuals

    Full legal names, titles, and contact information for owners, registered agents, officers, directors, or managing members who must appear on official filings or compliance documents.

  • Registered Agent Information

    The name and physical Washington State address of your registered agent. If you do not yet have one designated, we can advise on your options during the preparation process.

  • Agency Notices & Correspondence

    Any letters, notices, or communications received from the Washington Secretary of State, Department of Revenue, or other regulatory agencies — especially those referencing compliance deadlines or penalties.

  • Filing Deadlines & Time-Sensitive Dates

    Any known renewal dates, annual report due dates, or court-imposed deadlines. Sharing these upfront allows us to prioritize your file and ensure nothing is missed during preparation.

Not every item on this list applies to every engagement. If you are unsure what to bring, contact us before your consultation — we are happy to clarify exactly what your specific filing requires.
